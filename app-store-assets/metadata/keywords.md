## Why these keywords

- **`date`, `date night`, `date ideas`** — core category, high search volume
- **`couples`, `dating`, `relationship`** — adjacent categories Apple Search Ads bids on
- **`AI date`** — captures the category-new searcher who's heard about AI dating tools
- **`romantic`** — emotional adjective with steady search
- **`NYC date`** — geo-modifier; we lead with NYC at launch and capture the city searcher
- **`plan a date`** — long-tail action phrase, lower competition, higher intent

## Keywords NOT to include

These overlap with App Name or Subtitle and Apple counts them twice — wasted character spend:
- "Your Date Genie" (in Name)
- "AI Dates" (in Subtitle)
- "Real Romance" (in Subtitle)

## Words you can NEVER use in Apple keywords (will reject)

- Trademarked competitors: Hinge, Bumble, Tinder, Match, etc.
- "Free" — Apple strips this automatically
- "Best" / "Top" — claim words
- Other languages — keywords field is English-only for the US store

## Post-launch keyword test plan

After week 1 in the store, look at App Store Connect → Trends → which search terms drove installs. Swap the lowest-performing two for the next two on this shortlist:
- `swipe`, `match`, `couple goals`, `valentine`, `anniversary`, `wine bar`, `date night NYC`, `partner`, `lover`, `weekend plans`
