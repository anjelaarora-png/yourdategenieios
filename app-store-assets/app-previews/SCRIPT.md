## Recording method

**Option A — Real device + QuickTime:**
1. Plug iPhone into Mac via USB.
2. Open QuickTime → File → New Movie Recording → click arrow next to red record → select iPhone.
3. Walk through the app following the storyboard timing.
4. Export at 886×1920 portrait via Final Cut Pro or iMovie.

**Option B — Simulator:**
1. Open Xcode → Simulator → iPhone 16 Pro Max.
2. File → Record Screen.
3. Walk through the app.
4. Export and resize to 886×1920.

**Option C (fastest if no time): Lottie animation only:**
- Build the whole sequence in After Effects or Lottie based on the storyboard.
- No real device needed.
- I can produce the Lottie JSON if you authenticate Canva or hand off the After Effects template.

## What App Store reviewers look for in App Previews

1. **Real app footage** — no fake mockups, no After Effects fakes that don't match the live app.
2. **Visible app icon at some point** — usually the cold open or close.
3. **No competitor mentions, no "best" claims** without proof.
4. **Working hands-on flow** — reviewers want to see real taps, not edited cuts that hide states.
5. **Caption text legible at small size** — Apple plays this at thumbnail size in search.

The 0:12–0:18 hero moment is the single most important part of the video. The reveal of the actual date plan is what converts. Spend the majority of your edit time on that segment.
